Cryptography Beginner Maze

Level 4: Hash Cracking

In this mind-bending mission, you will step into the role of a budding cybersecurity expert. Your task is to unravel the secrets of intercepted hashed messages. These messages, encoded with algorithms like MD5, SHA-256, or Base64, hide crucial information. Can you use your skills to decode the mystery and reveal the plaintext? Gear up to tackle cryptic clues, leverage powerful tools, and embrace the challenge of cracking the code!

Introduction:

John and Mike are two friends who aren't very tech-savvy. They want to share a secret message online but think using hashed passwords will protect their conversation. They don’t know much about hash functions, so they pick one they found on a random website to "secure" their messages

Hashing:

Encryption Method: MD5, SHA-256, and Base64 Hashing

Encrypted Messages:


Alex, an expert hacker, intercepts their hashed data. Alex knows how to use powerful online tools like CrackStation and Xorbin to crack weak hashes.

Objective:

Your objective is to understand the vulnerabilities of using weak hashing algorithms like MD5 and how they can be cracked using online tools. By completing this challenge, you will enhance your understanding of hash functions and their security risks.

Prerequisites:

  • Basic knowledge of cryptographic functions, particularly hashing algorithms
  • Familiarity with online hash-cracking tools like CrackStation and Xorbin

Assignment:

  1. Learn about Hashing Algorithms: Understand what a hash function is and how different algorithms like MD5 are used to "secure" messages.
  2. Crack the Hash: Using online tools like CrackStation or Xorbin, decrypt the provided hashes
  3. Understand the Risks: Reflect on the ease with which online tools can crack weak hashes and why stronger encryption methods should be used for sensitive data.
